Japanese

Overview

Japanese is a difficult language to get started with but easier than you would expect.

first you should learn and memorize the writing systems Hiragana and Katakana along with general pronunciation. There is a third writing system, Kanji, which you will spend your whole life learning, so don't stress over it too much. :)

The Syllabary "Gojuuon"

As stated before, Japanese has Three writing systems. There is no Alphabet unless you count Romaji which is just English letters, but Romaji serves little purpose in actual Japanese.

Hiragana and Katakana are mostly just mirrors of each other. They represent the same sounds, but are used for slightly different purposes. Hiragana is used mostly for grammar points, but is also used for certain native Japanese words. Katakana on the other hand is either used for foreign loan words or to indicate vulgarity in certain instances.

Both Hiragana and Katakana both represent the Japanese Syllablary, the Gojuuon「五十音」 or the "Fifty Sounds". Each character in the Gojuuon represents a syllable and not a letter. The difference is that a syllable is like a consonant and vowel combined, but a letter is either a representation of an individual consonant or vowel. In Japanese the syllables also do not have names separate from their pronunciation. In other words, the name of each syllable is the same as its pronunciation.

Kanji

The third writing system of Japanese is Kanji. Kanji is used to represent most words in Japanese. It is neither an alphabet nor a syllabary meaning you must memorize how each word or reading in context. Kanji stands for ideas and not sounds. While I did state that kanji does not represent a sound, there are different readings associated with the kanji in different contexts
